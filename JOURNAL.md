## Apr 28
this was a whole four days after the 2026-27 game was revealed (watch the reveal [here](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=68NxYIAzbkY) if you so desire) so nobody really knew what to do... this time was mainly me talking with people and thinking cuz the amount of time it took to actually make the dt was super small since i can use a parametric drive train generator i previously made over the off season 
decided on a 30x30 450rpm 2.75in wheel 4 motor drive like this for a couple reasons:

1. Usually we would do 450rpm 3.25in wheel dt like last season which would actually be a lot higher linear speed actually pushing the robot due to the bigger wheels, but this year Vex made a new rule limiting ppl to 55w instead of the 66w+ that was typical last year... also robots are gna be hella heavy this year compared to last because robots have to reach up to 50in high. therefore with this extra weight and less power the remaining motors would burn out fast and possibly get damaged so u gotta make it slower to compensate
2. 30x30 is hella big compared to last year, but that makes sense cuz once again, robots are gonna reach up 50in tall. its gonna be hard to keep steady when a significant amount of weight is 4 feet up. soo u gotta make the plane of contact on the ground larger to compensate. 
3. technically 55w is 5 motors (there are half power motors u can use) which would be 2.5 on each side instead of the 2 we have now (vex sells half power motors u can use that are weaker and shittier) but according to our mentors using the half power motors on each side is actually a buns idea cuz they burn out faster which causes your whole drive to stop working when the half power ones burn out but the full power ones are still fine. therefore not doin that 44w good enoygh for me
4. uhh i thought there was smth else but i forgot
